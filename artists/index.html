Burna Boy

Burna Boy enters the atlas through Dai Dai, the official FIFA World Cup 2026 song performed with Shakira. The profile is deliberately narrow: it documents the tournament credit, its place in the 2026 music program, and the sources needed to distinguish confirmed status from promotional or fan language.

Davido

Davido connects two consecutive World Cup cycles in the atlas. He is credited on Hayya Hayya (Better Together), the first single from Qatar 2022's multi-song official soundtrack, and on No Place Like Home, an official-album track in the 2026 collection.

Jung Kook

Jung Kook appears through Dreamers, a Qatar 2022 official-soundtrack release connected directly to the tournament's opening. The atlas pairs the recording with Fahad Al Kubaisi's ceremony credit and keeps the page centred on that documented World Cup moment.

K'naan

K'naan's atlas profile is built around Wavin' Flag, a 2010 campaign song that became inseparable from many fans' World Cup memories. Its inclusion also illustrates an important editorial distinction: cultural recognition does not automatically make a track FIFA's main official tournament song.

Nora Fatehi

Nora Fatehi appears in two atlas entries across consecutive tournament cycles: Light The Sky from the Qatar 2022 official soundtrack and Siir Siir from the 2026 official album. Together they form a compact example of FIFA's shift toward larger, multi-artist music programs.

Pitbull's World Cup entry in the atlas is We Are One (Ole Ola), the official song associated with Brazil 2014 and performed with Jennifer Lopez and Claudia Leitte. The collaboration anchors his profile in a specific organiser-labelled release rather than in general football-themed music.

Ricky Martin

Ricky Martin's atlas entry centres on The Cup of Life from the 1998 tournament cycle. It is one of the site's historical reference points for the era when a single multilingual pop release could become the most recognisable musical shorthand for an entire World Cup.

Shakira

Shakira is one of the clearest multi-tournament links in this atlas. Her entries connect the 2010 South Africa cycle, where Waka Waka became a defining official-song memory, with the 2026 cycle through Dai Dai, a new official song credited jointly with Burna Boy.
